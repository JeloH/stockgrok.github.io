Future Opportunities

Voice User Interface

While b7g is a big power up in allowing people with visual impairments to access context and details in financial visualizations, we envision a future where people true nonlinear access to information is a reality. A user would simply ask a voice user interface to bring them exactly where they need to be on a page. Such a system would also be capable of performing comparisons and doing math for the user, to minimize cognitive overload. Challenges would include keeping the user informed of where they are on a page, or how they might toggle back through nonlinear exploration.

Multiple Technical Indicators

Having access to technical indicators is a must for professionals who employ technical analysis as a part of their investment strategy. While hearing the distance from one moving average is a major step up in making more confident predictions about future price movements, many sighted technical analysts are comparing price to multiple moving averages, or other indicators, entirely. These simultaneous comparisons are done in part to establish levels of support and resistance (i.e. price floors and ceilings), and help people create more compelling and grounded arguments. We envision a solution that goes beyond playing multiple sonified lines in stereo to help chunk multiple line comparisons at once.

Desktop Scrubbing

The scrubbing motion on smartphones is immensely powerful for people with visual impairments because unlike a computer screen, position on a phone is fixed. Unfortunately, this technology doesn’t currently translate very well to desktop in an industry where simultaneous access to multiple screens worth of information and desktop computing power is needed to effectively do one’s job. As such, people with visual impairments still struggle with the same positioning issues, or stick to linear navigation via screen readers.. We want to make a call to the world of emerging technologies to create a desktop answer to mobile scrubbing. In the interim, hooking up a phone to a computer might represent a step in the right direction, but the relative positioning problem of computer is not going to go away.

Haptic Graphs

Ultimately, sound is just one way of transcribing visual data in another modality. We spoke with some very forward-thinking researchers at Carnegie Mellon who are making great strides towards the ultimate goal of being able to dynamically simulate shapes and textures. We believe that dynamically generated haptic graphs would revolutionize not only the access people with visual impairments have to financial information, but anything else in their physical or digital environment, too. From a finance lens, it is currently possible to 3D print tactile graphs; however, there is as of yet no solution that reconciles the detail a financial graph would have to convey, with the on-the-fly speed at which financial professionals, especially day traders, need access to real time information.

Team b7g

We are a team of five Carnegie Mellon University Master’s of Human Computer Interaction students. We have been working with Bloomberg, LP to help make financial visualizations more accessible to people with visual impairments. This product is the culmination of eight months of research and design.
